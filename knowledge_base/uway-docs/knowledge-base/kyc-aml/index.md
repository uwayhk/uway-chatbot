# KYC/AML Guide

**Know Your Customer and Anti-Money Laundering compliance framework.**

## Introduction

KYC (Know Your Customer) and AML (Anti-Money Laundering) procedures are fundamental requirements for financial institutions and regulated businesses. This guide covers best practices for implementing compliant KYC/AML workflows.

## Core Components

### 1. Customer Identification Program (CIP)

**Required Documents:**
- Government-issued ID (Passport, HKID, National ID)
- Proof of Address (utility bill, bank statement < 3 months)
- Business registration documents (for corporate entities)

**Verification Methods:**
- Document authenticity checks (security features, expiry)
- Facial recognition and liveness detection
- Database cross-referencing (PEP, sanctions lists)

### 2. Risk-Based Approach

**Risk Categories:**

| Risk Level | Due Diligence | Review Frequency |
|------------|---------------|------------------|
| Low | Simplified DD | Every 2 years |
| Medium | Standard DD | Annually |
| High | Enhanced DD | Every 6 months |

**High-Risk Indicators:**
- PEP (Politically Exposed Persons)
- High-risk jurisdictions
- Complex ownership structures
- Cash-intensive businesses

### 3. Ongoing Monitoring

- Transaction monitoring for suspicious patterns
- Periodic KYC refresh and document updates
- Adverse media screening
- Sanctions list re-screening

### 4. Record Keeping

- Minimum 5 years retention (HK AMLO Cap.615)
- Secure storage with audit trails
- Access controls and encryption

## Hong Kong Requirements

**AMLO (Cap. 615) Key Provisions:**
- Customer due diligence obligations
- Record-keeping requirements
- Reporting suspicious transactions
- Staff training requirements

## Singapore Requirements

**MAS Notice 626:**
- Risk-based CDD measures
- Enhanced measures for higher risk
- Ongoing monitoring obligations

## Implementation Checklist

- [ ] Define risk assessment methodology
- [ ] Establish CIP document requirements
- [ ] Implement automated verification tools
- [ ] Set up sanctions/PEP screening
- [ ] Create ongoing monitoring workflows
- [ ] Document policies and procedures
- [ ] Train compliance staff
